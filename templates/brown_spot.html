Brown Spot

Description

Brown Spot is a fungal disease affecting the leaves of rice plants, caused by the fungus Bipolaris oryzae. The disease is characterized by small, circular to oval lesions with a gray center and dark brown margins on the leaves. Brown Spot is most prevalent in nutrient-deficient soils, particularly those low in potassium, and in areas with poor water management. If severe, the disease can cause leaf drying, reduced photosynthesis, and ultimately, lower yields.
