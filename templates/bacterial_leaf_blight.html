Bacterial Leaf Blight

(Xanthomonas oryzae pv. Oryzae)

Description

Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB) is a serious disease affecting rice plants, caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae. It is most prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions and can lead to significant yield losses, particularly in areas with high humidity and frequent rainfall. The disease typically starts with water-soaked lesions at the leaf tips, which expand downwards along the leaf margins, eventually turning yellow and then brown. If left untreated, BLB can cause the leaves to dry up completely, leading to stunted plant growth and reduced grain production.
