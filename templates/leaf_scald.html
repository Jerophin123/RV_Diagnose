Leaf Scald

Description

Leaf Scald is characterized by the drying of leaf tips and margins, leading to large, irregularly shaped necrotic areas on the leaves. The disease is caused by the fungus Gerlachia oryzae and is commonly found in areas with high humidity and frequent rainfall. Leaf Scald can severely affect photosynthesis by damaging large portions of the leaf surface, leading to reduced plant growth and yield.
