Sheath Blight

Description

Sheath Blight is a fungal disease causing lesions on the sheaths of rice plants, caused by the pathogen Rhizoctonia solani. The disease is most severe in areas with high humidity and dense plantings, which create conditions conducive to the spread of the fungus. Sheath Blight can lead to reduced grain filling, lower yields, and increased lodging if not managed properly.
