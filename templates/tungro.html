Tungro

Description

Tungro is a viral disease transmitted by leafhoppers, particularly the green leafhopper, and it is caused by the interaction of two viruses: Rice Tungro Spherical Virus (RTSV) and Rice Tungro Bacilliform Virus (RTBV). Infected plants exhibit stunted growth, yellow-orange leaf discoloration, and poor grain development, leading to significant yield losses. Tungro is most prevalent in Southeast Asia and is considered one of the most destructive rice diseases in the region.
