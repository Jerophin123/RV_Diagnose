Rice Hispa

Description

Rice Hispa is an insect pest that causes damage to rice leaves, characterized by the scraping of leaf tissue, which leaves a white streak on the leaf surface. The adult beetles feed on the upper leaf surface, while the larvae mine the leaf tissue, causing significant damage to the photosynthetic area of the plant. Severe infestations can lead to reduced plant vigor, delayed maturity, and lower yields.
