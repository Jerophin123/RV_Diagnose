Leaf Blast

Description

Leaf Blast is a common disease in rice, caused by the fungus Magnaporthe oryzae. It is one of the most destructive diseases in rice cultivation worldwide. The disease first appears as small, water-soaked lesions on the leaves, which enlarge and develop gray centers with brown borders. Severe infections can lead to significant yield losses as the lesions expand, causing leaf death and, in some cases, the complete destruction of the crop.
