Neck Blast

Description

Neck Blast affects the neck of the rice panicle and is caused by the same pathogen responsible for Leaf Blast, Magnaporthe oryzae. The disease can cause significant yield losses by affecting the panicle neck, leading to incomplete grain filling and empty grains. Neck Blast is particularly damaging when it occurs during the early heading stage, as it can result in the complete loss of the panicle.
